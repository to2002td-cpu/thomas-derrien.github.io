Research

My research focuses on methods for linking named entities in ambiguous multimodal corpora. In multimodal entity linking, the objective is to map a mention in unstructured data to its corresponding entity in a knowledge base by leveraging both textual and visual information. Although combining modalities introduces additional complexity, it also provides complementary signals that can resolve ambiguities left open by text alone.

I am particularly interested in retrieval-oriented representations that make this process accurate, scalable, and efficient. Multimodal embeddings and vision-language models are powerful tools for this task, but they often come with significant computational costs. Developing methods that efficiently index and retrieve relevant entities while maintaining strong performance is a central focus of my work.

Publications

Selected publications.

2026

FAST-MEL: A Fast, Accurate, and Storage Efficient Solution for Multimodal Entity Linking

Thomas Derrien, Laurent Amsaleg, Pascale Sébillot

ACM SIGIR 2026 · Short Paper

An encoder-based approach to multimodal entity linking that represents text and image mentions as compact fixed-size vectors. FAST-MEL matches the linking accuracy of state-of-the-art systems while running orders of magnitude faster and requiring an order of magnitude less storage.
